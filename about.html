Made for the night.
Built for you.

College nightlife is broken. I decided it was time to build something better.

Better Nights began as a project for the Oregon Innovation Challenge. As a student at the University of Oregon, I kept seeing the same disconnect. Weekends were supposed to be about letting go, but they were full of friction. Students were wandering around looking for the right crowd or walking into places that totally missed the mark. More importantly, I saw my own friends struggling to find a secure way home.

Most social platforms want to keep you glued to a screen. I wanted to build something that actually brings people together in the real world.

Find your people. Instantly.

Finding the right room should never be a guessing game. Your Friday night deserves better than aimless walking.

Better Nights connects you with the exact environment you want. Whether you are looking for a crowded dance floor or a quiet place to meet new people, the app cuts through the noise. It puts you exactly where you want to be, surrounded by the right energy.

Your safety. In your hands.

The walk home matters just as much as the party itself. When talking to other students, the same story kept coming up. Friends were feeling anxious, unsafe, or lost while navigating off campus at night.

Just finding a party is not enough. The platform had to actually protect you. That is why Better Nights includes an active map driven by real time reporting. You can see blocked routes, hazards, and campus activity before you step outside. And if you ever feel unsafe, a single button press sends your live location straight to your trusted circle of friends. The people you rely on most will know exactly where you are.

Privacy. It is a right.

Your safety should never require giving up your personal data.

Better Nights is built on uncompromising privacy. We do not sell your location. We do not stockpile your digital footprint. When you trigger an SOS alert, your coordinates go exclusively to the friends you explicitly select. The people you do not want to have your location simply do not get it.

I built this app so you can focus on the music, the people, and the moment. Leave the rest to us.
